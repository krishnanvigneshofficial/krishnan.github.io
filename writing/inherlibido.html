In My Libido


By
Krishnan Vignesh
In a day surrounded Dusk and Hail, Men stimulated by the nature of Gloth and Lust. Frozen and vexed by the mystic river termed “Affection”, We lived something as a heist, And now aimed by a weapon termed “Temptation”.

This nature, beyond the air and mist, Where life is just an aversion, Love is but a tender leaf, That reprises the one below it even at its eternal rest By falling over another life below it. Flying in the breeze to reach the people in attic, Waiting for nature to take their gloom.

In the beauty of Dusky-Blues, I could feel a piece of leaf striving for its last wish A Jane surrounded by Skanks, Overwhelmed by concupiscence, With an attitude of a Lackluster, Attacked on her sexually. And she came to me, Bare as a baby with Voluptuous melancholy, Confessing herself as a trollop.
Striving to be in hunger trying to satisfy my mate’s hunger, She pleaded for some grains of leftovers. Of a pity, I offered her some cookies from my cupboard And stirred some strew for her.

In a terrible hunger, having finished the bowl of stew, I felt repugnant being unable to satisfy her hunger. Awaiting my children and the need to leave was her situation. Which made me know about her. There began an anecdote in her Latency

Striving for nourishment and entangled by myths, Stranded in a locus to wear a nuptial thread by my blood relation. Knowing the pressure arising from my family, Eloping was the desirable resolution she found.

Standing still in beneath the tree of fortune With the tender leaves carried away from me by the wind And still dispersing aside my legs in a feeble manner. The age of latency for a girl and incomplete desires Awoke a libido in me which made me a scarlet woman. The nature forced to use my beauty to satisfy lack luster’s hunger, And now making me plea for a nourishment of my children.

With the drive and desire of nature, The last leaf from the tree of fortune fell Cascading on my lap holding my children’s head and dreams Hoping to cherish in the moment of eternity.
